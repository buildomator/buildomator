<objective>
Suppress the deprecation nudge in the generated `bm` package (D-06): bm must never tell
users to switch to bm. Single-source the suppression through the existing
`bin/lib/bm-transform.cjs` build transform (the same mechanism that keeps gsd/bm from
drifting), add it to the `build-bm.cjs generate()` per-file loop, regenerate `dist/bm`, and
prove with a test that the gsd source still emits the nudge (including when yielding) while
`dist/bm` carries no nudge. The `--check` drift gate then enforces the suppression forever.

Critical self-corruption hazard to close BEFORE execution: `bm-transform.cjs` embeds the
`// BM-NUDGE-START` / `// BM-NUDGE-END` sentinel literals (they DEFINE the strip), and the
nudge test embeds `BM-NUDGE`. A non-anchored suppressNudge applied to its own source during
generate() would delete everything between the two literals in `dist/bm/bin/lib/bm-transform.cjs`,
silently corrupting the transform in the bm package. Mirror the existing `STAMP_EXCLUDE`
precedent in build-bm.cjs (a SUPPRESS_EXCLUDE set) AND line-anchor the sentinel match, and
add `node --check dist/bm/bin/lib/bm-transform.cjs` to the acceptance gate so any
self-corruption fails loudly.

Purpose: bm stays silent on the nudge; gsd keeps it; the two provably cannot diverge and the
transform cannot corrupt itself.
Output: a self-protected suppressNudge transform, wired build, regenerated dist/bm, and a nudge test.
</objective>
